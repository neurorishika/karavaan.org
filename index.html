GREEN SCREEN

A game that comprises of a lot of fun, for both the participants as well as the audience.

RULES

  1. The participant is blindfolded and must attempt guessing a video playing in the background.
  2. The video will be played (and replayed) for a time-window of two minutes.
  3. Within this time, the judges will suggest what the video is about by making witty puns.
  4. The participant should converse with the judges and try to guess the video or what it is about within the two minutes.
  5. During the conversation, coherence should be maintained, and statements shouldn't be inserted randomly.
  6. The videos cover the genres of science fiction, current affairs in science and pop-culture.

BLOCK AND TACKLE

How quickly can you change sides? How strongly can you contradict yourself? How often do you find yourself in a situation supporting two opposing viewpoints? If you think you have these superpowers, come join us for Block and Tackle as we will uncover the hidden superpower of everyone to speak with self-contradicting statements!

RULES

  1. You will be given a topic 2 minutes beforehand.
  2. You will have to speak for 3 minutes continuously to either support or refute it .
  3. Whenever the judges say "Tackle", you will have to speak against the topic. While if they say "Block", you will have to continue supporting the topic.
  4. You will be judged on how well you can contradict yourself and how well you can cross change sides midway!

Leadership Conclave

The Panel discussion on the topic - 'Budget in basic science: from a cash-crunched past to the future forwardâ€™, is to be held on Thursday, November 1, 2018, from 7 PM at the Sir CV Raman Auditorium, IISER Pune.
The primary intent behind putting together this panel of speakers from a wide range of backgrounds is to provide a common platform for all the diverse perspectives to weigh in into those policy decisions which determine the course of scientific progress in our country.
By dissecting this theme, we seek to discuss and bring forth into the public theatre the following issues as starters :-

  • A brief analysis of the current scenario in funding of basic sciences.
  • Usage of the taxpayer's money to fund what one may term "socially irrelevant" research.
  • Parallels between a society's progress and a flourishing scientific community.
  • Scope of and qualms surrounding industrial funding of research in the basic sciences.
  • Future of research in the basic sciences in India with current gears in place
  • The potential of a self-sustaining scientific community in India and a roadmap to achieving that.
The Panel will be composed of five members (from Industry, Academia and Administration) and will commence with ten-minute talks by everyone on their varied opinions on the nuances of the matter at hand. The main discussion will follow thereafter, and we intend to close with a session of open interaction with the audience.
Please find below the details of Confirmed Speakers for Leadership Conclave in addition to the topic and format shared above:

  1. Prof Sandhya Visveswariah: Scientist and academic at the Indian Institute of Science (IISc) Bangalore, India. She is currently the Chairperson of the Department of Molecular Reproduction, Development and Genetics and the Co-chair of the Centre for Biosystems Science and Engineering, IISc Bangalore.
  2. Prof Sabyasachi Bhattacharya: He is a CV Raman University Professor at Ashoka University. He is a Distinguished Professor at the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR), Mumbai of which he was the former Director. Apart from Physics, he is interested in Higher Education Policy in India related especially to science and technology research.
  3. Dr Rajeev Sharma: Retired in July 2018 from Government of India as the Secretary, Science and Engineering Research Board, India as well as Head, Technology Missions in the Department of Science and Technology related to research, development, and demonstration in areas of Water, Clean Energy and Supercomputing. Previously headed the bilateral Indo-US Science and Technology Forum as Executive Director. He is currently the Director General, Amity Foundation for Science, Technology and Innovation Alliances.
  4. Prof Sanjeev Galande: Dean, Research and Development and Chair, Department of Biology at IISER Pune. He is an elected fellow of the Indian National Science Academy and the Indian Academy of Sciences and a recipient of the National Bioscience Award for Career Development of the Department of Biotechnology. He was also awarded the Shanti Swarup Bhatnagar Prize for Science and Technology in 2010, for his contributions to biological sciences.
The Panel Discussion will be moderated by Prof Sunil Mukhi, former Chair, Department of Physics, IISER Pune. Formerly, he was Professor of Physics at TIFR Mumbai. He is a Fellow of the Indian Academy of Sciences, Indian National Science Academy and The World Academy of Sciences, and a recipient of the Shanti Swarup Bhatnagar Award for Physical Sciences, 1999 and the Jagdish Chandra Bose Fellowship, 2008.

About

Karavaan is the annual science, cultural, and social fest of IISER Pune. Born in 2008 as a small intra-college festival, it has grown with each edition and has now matured into something far greater.

The 11th edition of Karavaan is scheduled to occur on 2nd, 3rd and 4th of November, 2018. These three days will be sprawling with a plethora of competitions and activities spanning areas like music, dance, photography, science, quizzing, drama, literary, and general tomfoolery. Eminent personalities from all over India from niche fields will also be invited to join us in a number of much-awaited talk series and workshops on a variety of subjects on the days leading up to, as well as during Karavaan. The theme for this year, "Fandoms and Fiction" , will in no small measure, leave a mark on the minds of everyone who joins us for the fest that is Karavaan.
